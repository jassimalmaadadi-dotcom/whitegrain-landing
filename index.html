The problem

Most construction surprises are hidden in vague quotes.

A contractor quote can look complete while still missing the details that decide the real cost, quality, and payment risk.

“Tiles included” No brand, size, allowance, supplier, installation rule, or credit if you choose cheaper tiles.
“AC lump sum” No brand, tonnage, duct insulation, testing, commissioning, or upgrade rule.
“Waterproofing included” No system, membrane type, installation method, warranty, or inspection requirement.
“Payment stage” Payment is requested, but the quote does not say what work must be completed first.

What it checks

WhiteGrain is organized around real homeowner decisions.

Instead of a generic document scan, the app will focus on the decisions you actually need to make before choosing a contractor.

Cost

Compare the quote against a rough Qatar cost range and see which categories drive the budget.

BOQ

What is missing from the BOQ?

Find missing quantities, vague descriptions, lump sums, exclusions, and unrealistic allowances.

Specs

Are the technical specs clear?

Check concrete, waterproofing, cable specs, AC insulation, paint, and workmanship details.

Materials

Check brands, tile allowance, supplier choice, upgrade cost, substitutions, and price difference rules.

Contract
